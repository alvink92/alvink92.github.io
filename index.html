About Me

Hey, nice to meet you! I'm a UCSB Physics grad/Data Analyst turned Software Developer who loves code maintainability, scalability, and efficiency. I was first introduced to coding in one of my physics labs, where I used Python to parse through a bunch of position vs time data, taken from our Gravitation Torsion Balance, to analyze the gravitational constant in Mathematica. I was later exposed to a little bit of C in my digital electronics course, where I used it to write layers of logic for my Arduino project.

After graduating, I continued to develop my programming skills, mostly in Python, and landed a job as a Data Analyst at a small online retailer based in San Francisco. There, I created business intelligence reports and dashboards, forecasts for upcoming sale to aid in inventory replenishment plans, and tools to monitor up and coming trends.

As of now, I am a recent grad from AppAcademy looking for a full-time position in the technology space as a Software Engineer to utilize my analytical and problem solving abilities.
